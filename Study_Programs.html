BACHELOR (BA)

The Bachelor's degree program in Dutch Language, Literature, and Culture is a three-year program. It can be studied as a major or a minor. Inspired by the famous poem of Hendrik Marsman, which says "When I think of the Netherlands, I see great possibilities," this program offers just that—vast opportunities. If you want to master Dutch and pursue a career with it, this is the right field for you. You will learn the language from the basics, explore its grammar, and dive into the literature of this unique language. You will also gain insight into the cultural, historical, and political landscapes of the Netherlands and Belgium, and learn how to write academic texts in Dutch, work with specialized sources, and develop skills in professional communication and translation.

MASTER (MA)

The Translation Studies program focuses on developing the language skills necessary for transferring messages between languages. With professional training, you’ll be equipped to tackle translation challenges and navigate the complexities of intercultural and interlingual communication. From the start, you'll create your own translations, learning the art of text and discourse analysis. You'll develop a keen understanding of communication, allowing you to interpret meanings—often hidden in deeper layers of language. The program includes expert lectures and specialized seminars led by professional translators and interpreters, followed by practical exercises in various language combinations. You'll translate a wide range of texts, from EU directives and cooking recipes to film subtitles, medical information, philosophical essays, and technical specifications.
